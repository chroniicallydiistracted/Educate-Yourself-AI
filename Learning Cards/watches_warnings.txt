# Watches, Warnings & Advisories (Concept)
One-sentence takeaway: A watch highlights favorable conditions; a warning indicates an imminent or occurring hazard; advisories cover significant but lower-end impacts.

What it is:
Hazard communication categories used by meteorological services.

How it works:
Issued based on probability, lead time, and observed reports/radar signatures.

Key terms:
Severe thunderstorm/tornado watch vs warning; Flash flood watch vs warning.

What you’ll see on maps/layers:
Polygon overlays for active hazards; population and infrastructure layers for impact context.

Common misconceptions:
“Watch means it’s happening.” Watch = potential; Warning = occurring/imminent.

Related topics:
Nowcasting; Decision support; Risk categories.
